The Problem

The average consumer is producing more waste than ever before. We hope to empower teenagers and young adults by spreading awareness about the environmental impact of the fast fashion industry and the importance of sustainability.

Fast Facts:

  • The average consumer throws away 70 pounds of clothing per year.
  • 20% of industrial water pollution comes from textile treatment and dyes.
  • 1.5 trillion liters of water are used by the fashion industry each year.
  • The equivalent of 1 garbage truck of textiles is wasted every second.
  • 80 billion garments are produced every year.
  • An average of 35 kg of textile waste is generated per person each year in the US.
  • 20,000 people die of cancer and miscarriages every year as a
    result of chemicals sprayed on cotton.

The Solution

There are so many problems with fast fashion, but what are some ways YOU can help?

Some ways to be more sustainable:

  • Choose clothes made in countries with stricter environmental regulations for factories (EU, Canada, US, etc.)
  • Choose fibers with low water consumption such as linen, recycled fibers, etc.
